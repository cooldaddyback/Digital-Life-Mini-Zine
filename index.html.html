Authentic Enough

Exploring how we perform ourselves online, and how platforms quietly shape the versions of ourselves we think are “real.”

The Scroll

I wake up and instinctively scroll Instagram.

Half asleep, I peek at other people’s breakfasts, selfies, and tiny life updates.

Each post seems effortless, yet someone, somewhere, curated it carefully.

Even the “natural” photos are a performance.

The Performance

We all perform. Even when we don’t think we are.

Posting “in the moment” doesn’t guarantee authenticity.

Every post is framed, filtered, and timed.

Calculated Authenticity ‚Äî showing what you want to be seen as, rather than the full, messy reality.

Ritual Appreciation

Commenting “stunning <3” on friends’ selfies isn’t lying.

Ritual Appreciation ‚Äî we hype each other up online, socially performing care.

This isn’t inauthentic. It’s how we maintain our bonds in digital spaces. We signal affection through likes, comments, and emojis.

The performance of care is care.

The Silent Audience

Some people view my Stories without a word.

I notice. And I care, somehow.

Peripherally Connected ‚Äî the small, indirect ways we maintain social bonds online.

These viewers aren’t close friends. They’re part of our social orbit‚Äîwatching, existing, staying loosely connected.

It’s a new kind of relationship. Real, but at a distance.

The Break

Deleted Instagram for a week ‚Äî a Digital Sabbatical.

Day 1-3

Odd, loud, anxious. The phantom notifications. The urge to capture and share everything.

Day 4-7

Quiet brain space. Clarity. Real thoughts that aren’t performed.

Day 8

Returning is inevitable. We live in an Always-On world.

But something shifts. You realize how much mental real estate these platforms occupy.

Authentic Enough

Authenticity isn’t about being completely transparent online.

It’s about choosing which version of yourself fits each context.

Your online self can be real and performed at the same time ‚Äî and that’s okay.

You can be:

  • Playful on Instagram
  • Professional on LinkedIn
  • Raw on BeReal
  • Private in your drafts

All of these versions are you. None of them are lies.

That is authentic enough.
